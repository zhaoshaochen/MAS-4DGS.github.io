Motion-Aware Selective 4D Gaussian Splatting for Efficient Dynamic Scene Reconstruction

Jun Wang1, Shaochen Zhao1, Qi Liu1, Fanghui Zhang1, Chunxiao Li1, Shunzhou Wang1
1School of Artificial Intelligence, Henan University
Code

Abstract

High-quality dynamic 3D scene reconstruction with real-time rendering capability remains a central challenge in computer vision. Most existing 4D Gaussian splatting approaches model dynamic scenes by applying spatiotemporal deformations indiscriminately to all Gaussian primitives. However, real-world scenes typically exhibit motion sparsity, where only a few regions move (e.g., pedestrians) and most areas remain nearly static (e.g., buildings). Consequently, this full-scene deformation causes substantial computational redundancy, severely hindering both efficiency and optimization stability. To address these limitations, we propose Motion-Aware Selective 4D Gaussian Splatting. To eliminate this redundancy, we introduce a low runtime overhead motion prediction network that generates per-Gaussian motion mask at each time step. By restricting deformation exclusively to dynamic primitives, we reduce the dominant deformation-branch cost from scaling with all N primitives to scaling with only the K dynamic ones. Second, to ensure stable convergence under selective deformation, we design a self-calibrating two-stage training strategy that mitigates error accumulation and periodically restores global gradient coverage. Finally, to handle topological evolution, we propose a motion-aware densification and pruning mechanism that strictly aligns geometric updates with motion semantics. Extensive experiments show that, compared to state-of-the-art methods, our approach significantly reduces computational cost and accelerates convergence, while achieving promising performance accuracy, especially for sparse-motion scenes common in real-world applications.
